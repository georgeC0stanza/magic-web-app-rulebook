Setup


  1. Shuffle

    Each player shuffles his or her deck. Each player may then shuffle or cut their opponentsâ€™ decks.

  2. Determine turn order

    Players determine which player will go first (by flipping a coin, rolling dice, etc.).

  3. Draw 7 Cards

    Each player draws a number of cards equal to their hand size (hand sizes are seven cards unless otherwise modified). A player who is dissatisfied with their initial hand may take a mulligan.*

  4. 20 Life

    Each player starts with 20 life (some game variants may have different starting life totals) which they are responsible for keeping track of (via dice, pen & paper, etc.).

* To take a mulligan, the player first declares that they will take a mulligan. Then he or she shuffles his or her hand back into their deck and redraws a hand (This process can be repeated multiple times if desired.). For each mulligan a player takes, that player must put that many cards from their hand on the bottom of their deck. In games with 3+ players, players get one free mulligan.


Winning


A player wins the game when all of their opponents have been eliminated (Though uncommon, it is possible for no one to win.). A player is eliminated from the game when either of the following happens:

  1. His or her life total is reduced to 0 or below
  2. He or she has to draw a card but cannot because their library has no cards remaining, or
  3. He or she has ten or more poison counters, they lose the game. (A player receives a poison counter for each life lost from a source with infect.)

Additionally, certain cards may create alternate win or loss conditions that when met can lead to a player either winning or losing a game.


Turn Structure


Magic is a turn based game. Each turn contains 5 phases, three of which are broken down further into steps. Certain phases require certain turn based actions to be performed such as untapping permanents, drawing a card, etc.


Upkeep


At the beginning of each turn, the active player untaps his or her permanents and then draws a card. The first player of a two player game skips his or her first draw.



Main Phase


The player then enters the Main Phase where he or she is free to cast spells and activate abilities (as many/often as desired assuming the cost is paid). Any player can react to these spells and abilities by playing instant spells and activating their own abilities. He or she can also play a land (only once per turn) from their hand.



Combat


When ready, the active player can enter into the Combat Phase. This phase consists of 3 basic steps: attacking, blocking, and assigning damage. To attack, the active player announces which creatures will attack which players. Then each player getting attacked will declare what creatures they control will block which attacking creatures. (A player can only block those creatures which are specifically attacking them.) After this, all creatures deal damage equal to their power. Blocked creatures deal damage to blocking creatures while they deal damage to the blocked creature. Unblocked creatures deal damage to the defending player. (A creature is put in the graveyard if it is dealt damage equal to or greater than its toughness.)



Main Phase


After Combat, there is a second Main Phase. This phase is identical to the first Main Phase. (Remember you can only play one land per turn.)



End Phase


Finally comes the End Phase where all damage is removed from creatures and the active player discards down to 7 cards.

Game Actions



Tapping and Untapping


Tapping a card is to turn it sideways, indicating that it has been used for the turn. Some actions that require tapping:

  • Attacking: Creatures tap then they attack
  • Add Mana: Lands make mana when you tap them
  • Activating abilities: Some permanents are tapped as a cost for their activated abilities
