Playing the game



Setup


  1. Shuffle

    Each player shuffles his or her deck. Each player may then shuffle or cut their opponentsâ€™ decks.

  2. Determine turn order

    Players determine which player will go first (by flipping a coin, rolling dice, etc.).

  3. Draw 7 Cards

    Each player draws a number of cards equal to their hand size (hand sizes are seven cards unless otherwise modified). A player who is dissatisfied with their initial hand may take a mulligan. To take a mulligan, the starting player first declares that they will take a mulligan. He or she shuffles his or her hand back into their deck and redraws their hand (This process can be repeated multiple times if desired.). For each mulligan taken, that player must put that many cards from their hand on the bottom of their deck.

  4. 20 Life

    Each player starts with 20 life (some game variants may have different starting life totals) which they are responsible for keeping track of (via dice, pen & paper, etc.).


Winning


A player wins the game when all of their opponents have been eliminated (Though uncommon, it is possible for noone to win.). A player is eliminated from the game when either of the following happens:

  1. His or her life total is reduced to 0 or below
  2. He or she has to draw a card but cannot because their library has no cards remaining, or
  3. He or she has ten or more poison counters, they lose the game. (A player receives a poison counter for each life lost from a source with infect.)

Additionally, certain cards may create alternate win or loss conditions that when met can lead to a win.



Parts of the Turn



Structure


Magic is a turn based game in which players take alternating turns. Each turn contains 5 phases, three of which are broken down further into steps. Certain phases require certain turn based actions to be performed such as untapping permanents, drawing a card, etc.


Phases


Beginning Phase

The active player:

  • Untaps their permanents
  • Draws a card

Main Phase

The active player:

  • May play a land (only one land per turn)
  • Cast spells

Combat Phase

  • The active player declares attackers
  • Opponents declare blockers
  • Combat damage is dealt

Main Phase

The active player:

  • May play a land (only one land per turn)
  • Cast spells

End Phase

  • Damage is removed from all creatures
  • The active player discards down to seven cards
  • The active player passes the turn

For more information, see our Parts of the Turn interactive guide.


Game Actions



Tapping and Untapping


Tapping a card is to turn it sideways, indicating that it has been used for the turn. Some actions that require tapping:

  • Attacking: Creatures tap then they attack
  • Add Mana: Lands make mana when you tap them
  • Activating abilities: Some permanents are tapped as a cost for their activated abilities
